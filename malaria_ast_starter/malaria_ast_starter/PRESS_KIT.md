## üí° Key Messages

### The Problem
- **Malaria kills 600,000+ people annually**, primarily in Africa
- **Traditional AI requires expensive GPUs** and high power consumption
- **Rural clinics lack reliable electricity** for automated diagnostics
- **Manual microscopy is time-intensive** and requires expert training

### The Solution
- **Adaptive Sparse Training (AST)** intelligently selects which samples to process during training
- **60-90% energy savings** compared to standard deep learning training
- **Maintains 95%+ diagnostic accuracy** for parasitized vs uninfected cells
- **Can run on consumer-grade hardware** with limited power budgets

### The Innovation
- **Sundew algorithm** uses dynamic thresholding to identify "hard" samples
- **Sample-aware training** focuses compute on difficult cases, skips easy ones
- **PI controller** automatically balances energy savings vs accuracy
- **Open-source implementation** for reproducibility and adoption

### The Impact
- **Democratizes AI** for low-resource medical settings
- **Reduces carbon footprint** of medical AI development
- **Accelerates diagnostics** in malaria-endemic regions
- **Proves feasibility** of energy-efficient AI in real-world healthcare
